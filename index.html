Computer Parts

Central Processing Unit (CPU)

The brain of the machine has to be carefully chosen according to what applications are intended to work with the machine. Your CPU choice will impact your motherboard choice.

  • More

Motherboard

The central nervous system of the PC. Every component will plug into your motherboard, and interact with one another through the motherboard.

  • More

Memory (RAM)

RAM (Random Access Memory) is the system's way of storing data about to be processed, or frequently used by applications, on a medium that allows for very fast transfer rates.

  • More

Hard Disk Drive (HDD) / Solid State Drive (SSD)

The Hard Disk Drive holds lots of storage for cheap. The Solid State Drive is more expensive per gigabyte of storage, but is much faster in loading Windows and other applications, includiing games.

  • More

Video card (GPU)

The video card is the component driving your display. A better video card will help run games faster. Most laptops have integrated GPUs, which means that they are combined with the CPU; these integrated GPUs are not great at running games, but they save much more battery.

  • More

Power Supply Unit (PSU)

The primary function of a power supply is to convert electric current from a wall socket to the correct voltage, current, and frequency to power computer parts.
